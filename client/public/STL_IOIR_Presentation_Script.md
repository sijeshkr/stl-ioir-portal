## SLIDE 2: THE CHALLENGE
**[Visual: Problem statement with icons]**

### Script:

"Let's start with the reality you're facing.

**You're launching a new clinic in a competitive market.** But you have three unique challenges:

**Challenge 1: Awareness**

**Challenge 3: Referrals**

## SLIDE 3: THE STRATEGY - "AUTHORITY FIRST, REFERRALS PRIMARY"
**[Visual: Inverted funnel showing Authority → Education → Action]**

### Script:

"Most medical practices do social media backwards. They chase patients with ads and hope for appointments.

**We're doing the opposite.**

Our strategy is called **'Authority First, Referrals Primary'** - and it's based on a simple insight:

**One referring physician is worth 100 direct patients.**

If a medical oncologist sends you 2-3 liver cancer patients per month, that's 24-36 cases per year from ONE relationship. To get that same volume from direct patient marketing, you'd need to spend tens of thousands on ads.

**So our social media strategy prioritizes:**

**60% of effort → LinkedIn** - Building authority with referring physicians  
**30% of effort → Facebook** - Educating patients who research their options  
**10% of effort → Instagram** - Secondary patient awareness

This is intentional. We're not ignoring patients - we're just being realistic about where your revenue comes from.

**The three-tier approach:**

**Tier 1: AUTHORITY (Top of Funnel)**  
Establish Dr. Vaheesan as the physician's choice for complex IO cases.  
*Channels:* LinkedIn, physician networks, tumor boards

**Tier 2: EDUCATION (Middle of Funnel)**  
Answer the 'What is IO/IR?' question for patients and families.  
*Channels:* Facebook, website, YouTube

**Tier 3: ACTION (Bottom of Funnel)**  
Make it easy to refer patients or book appointments.  
*Channels:* Google Search, referral portal, direct outreach

This isn't just social media - it's a complete go-to-market strategy."

## APPENDIX: ANTICIPATED FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS

### Budget Questions

**Q: "Can we start with organic only and add paid later?"**  
A: You can, but you'll sacrifice 60-70% of your launch momentum. New pages get 2-5% organic reach. Paid promotion is how you break through. Recommendation: Start with paid, then reduce once you have 1,000+ followers.

**Q: "What's the ROI on the $2,000 investment?"**  
A: Conservative estimate: $2,000 → 100 appointment requests → 60 appointments → 30 completed cases → $150,000-300,000 revenue (based on $5,000-10,000 average case value) = 75-150x ROI. Even at 10% conversion, it's 7-15x ROI.

### Content Questions

**Q: "What if we don't have patient testimonials yet?"**  
A: That's expected for a new clinic. We'll focus on credentials, procedure education, and Dr. Vaheesan's expertise for the first 30 days. Post-launch, we'll implement a review request system (BirdEye) to collect testimonials.

**Q: "Can we talk about specific success rates or outcomes?"**  
A: Yes, as long as they're evidence-based and not misleading. Example: '85-95% tumor destruction rate for tumors <3cm' is fine (backed by research). 'We cure cancer' is not.

**Q: "What if Dr. Vaheesan isn't comfortable on camera?"**

**Q: "Does he need to respond to every LinkedIn comment?"**

### Timeline Questions

**Q: "How long until we see results?"**

## PRESENTATION TIPS

### Delivery Recommendations

1. **Keep it conversational** - This is a script, not a speech. Use your own words.

2. **Pause for questions** - Don't rush through. Let them digest each section.

3. **Show examples** - Pull up actual LinkedIn/Facebook posts to visualize.

4. **Address concerns proactively** - If you sense hesitation about budget, address it immediately.

5. **End with confidence** - You've done the work. The strategy is sound. Ask for the decision.

### What to Bring

- This script (printed or on tablet)
- The full strategy document (PDF)
- The Excel content calendar (on laptop to show)
- Sample post graphics (if created)
- Budget breakdown (one-pager)
- Success metrics dashboard (template)

### Time Management

- **Slides 1-6:** 8-10 minutes (Strategy overview)
- **Slides 7-11:** 6-8 minutes (Tactical details)
- **Slides 12-17:** 4-5 minutes (Action items and close)
- **Q&A:** 5-10 minutes

**Total: 20-30 minutes including questions**

### Red Flags to Watch For

If client says... | What it means... | How to respond...
---|---|---
"Let's start small and see" | They're risk-averse | Emphasize low cost vs. high potential ROI
"We don't have budget for ads" | They don't understand paid social | Show organic reach stats (2-5%) vs. paid (20-30%)
"Dr. Vaheesan is too busy" | They don't see his role as critical | Explain the network effect of his personal LinkedIn
"Can we just post when we have time?" | They don't value consistency | Show algorithm favorability for consistent posting
"We'll handle it internally" | They're not ready to commit | Offer to train their team on the strategy

### Closing Techniques

**If they're ready to commit:**  
"Great! Let's get started this week. I'll send you the asset request list today, and we'll have Week 1 graphics ready by Friday."

**If they're hesitant:**  
"I understand this is a big decision. What specific concerns can I address to help you feel confident moving forward?"

**If they need time:**  
"Absolutely. When can we reconvene to finalize the decision? Keep in mind we need to start by Feb 18 to maximize pre-launch awareness."

**If they say no:**  
"I respect that. If you change your mind or want to revisit this in 3-6 months, the strategy will still be here. In the meantime, I recommend at minimum: claim your Google Business Profile, set up social media pages (even if not posting), and start collecting patient reviews from Day 1."
